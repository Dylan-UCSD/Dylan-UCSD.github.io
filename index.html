What PID Values did you choose and why?
How did you adjust Speed & PID for the different tracks?

Our PID values varied using the potentiometers, but the constant
multipliers largely remained the same. The frequency track used
lots of Integral due to the length of it, while the drag race and loops
used mainly proportion and derivative, although to a greater degree
for the loop. In hindsight, a few more tests on the constant values would
have benefited our trials.

4-6 sentences on improvements made during the last week
and improvements you wish you made (or if given more time would have)

During the last week, we made several improvements. Initially,
we added a light shield for the photoresistor, equipped with seven
LED lights. However, we decided to remove four of these LEDs, as we
did not need seven. Additionally, we opted for a cleaner and more
organized space by eliminating the PCB where the photoresistors and
resistors were originally connected. And lastly, we added LEDs to
indicate when our robots were calibrating white and black surfaces.

Improvements we wished to make were to enhance the robot's ability
to read track turns by strategically spacing out the photoresistors.
Also make a neater appearance by tidying up the wiring using tape.
And lastly, increase the robot's speed by reducing its weight.
This could involve making the light shield lighter or optimizing
other parts of the 3D design more lighter for a faster performance.
